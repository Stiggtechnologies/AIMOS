# SECTION 1: FRONT DESK SOP

The front desk is the patient experience control center.

## Responsibilities

- Appointment scheduling
- Patient intake
- Billing and insurance
- Review requests
- Referral tracking

---

## SOP-FD-001: PATIENT ARRIVAL AND CHECK-IN

### Step 1 – Greeting (10 seconds)

**Script:**
> "Welcome to Alberta Injury Management South Commons. How can we help you today?"

**Actions:**
- Make eye contact
- Smile genuinely
- Use warm, confident tone

### Step 2 – Confirm Appointment (15 seconds)

**Verify in AIM OS:**
- Patient name
- Appointment time
- Assigned clinician

**If patient is early:**
> "You're a bit early—please have a seat and we'll be with you shortly."

**If patient is late:**
> "No problem, let me check if we can still see you today."

### Step 3 – First Visit Intake (5 minutes)

**For NEW patients only:**

1. **Provide digital intake form**
   - Open AIM OS patient intake module
   - Send form to patient's phone OR provide tablet

2. **Forms include:**
   - Contact information
   - Injury description and history
   - Insurance provider details
   - Medical history
   - Consent for treatment
   - Privacy consent

3. **Collect key information:**
   - "What brings you in today?"
   - "Do you have extended health insurance?"
   - **CRITICAL:** "How did you hear about us?" (referral tracking)

### Step 4 – Insurance Verification (2 minutes)

**Actions:**
1. Ask: "Do you have extended health coverage?"
2. If YES:
   - Confirm insurance provider
   - Note coverage details
   - Explain: "We can bill your insurance directly if you'd like"
3. If NO:
   - Explain payment options
4. Enter insurance info in AIM OS billing module

**Common Insurers:**
- Sun Life
- Canada Life
- Manulife
- Blue Cross
- Green Shield
- Medavie

### Step 5 – Referral Source Tracking (30 seconds)

**CRITICAL for marketing:**

Ask: "How did you hear about us?"

**Record in AIM OS:**
- Trainer referral (get trainer name)
- Google search
- Friend/family referral
- Corporate/employer
- WCB
- Walk-in from gym

### Step 6 – Notify Clinician (15 seconds)

**Actions:**
1. Mark patient "Arrived" in AIM OS
2. Notify assigned physiotherapist
3. Tell patient: "The physiotherapist will be with you in about 5 minutes"

### Step 7 – Patient Waiting

**Standards:**
- Offer water
- Ensure comfortable seating
- Keep wait times under 10 minutes

# SECTION 2: PHYSIOTHERAPIST SOP

Clinicians must follow a consistent treatment model.

---

## SOP-CL-001: INITIAL ASSESSMENT PROTOCOL

### Step 1 – Patient Introduction (2 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Tell me about the issue you're experiencing and what activities are most affected."

**Listen carefully. Build rapport.**

### Step 2 – Assessment (15 minutes)

**Perform:**
- Movement analysis
- Range of motion testing
- Strength testing
- Pain assessment (VAS scale)
- Functional limitations

**Record findings in AIM OS**

### Step 3 – Diagnosis Explanation (5 minutes)

**Explain clearly:**
- What the injury is
- Why it happened
- Recovery timeline

**Patients must understand their condition**

### Step 4 – Treatment Plan (8 minutes)

**Define:**
- Number of visits expected (be realistic)
- Treatment approach
- Exercise rehabilitation plan
- Expected milestones

**Document in AIM OS**

---

## SOP-CL-002: TREATMENT SESSION STANDARD PROTOCOL

**Total time:** 45-50 minutes

### Standard Treatment Components:

1. **Manual therapy** (15-20 min)
   - Soft tissue work
   - Joint mobilization
   - Myofascial release

2. **Exercise rehabilitation** (20-25 min)
   - Corrective exercises
   - Strengthening exercises
   - Movement pattern correction

3. **Movement education** (5-10 min)
   - Body mechanics
   - Prevention strategies
   - Home program review

**Use gym floor when appropriate**

---

## SOP-CL-003: EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION AND GYM INTEGRATION

### Inside AIM OS:

**Assign:**
- Corrective exercises
- Strengthening exercises
- Mobility work

**Exercise Selection:**
- Appropriate for injury stage
- Progressive difficulty
- Gym equipment when suitable

### Gym Floor Usage:

**When to use:**
- Patient is stable
- Exercises require gym equipment
- Functional movement training

**Gym etiquette:**
- Respect other gym members
- Clean equipment after use
- Stay within clinic area when possible

---

## SOP-CL-004: SESSION DOCUMENTATION IN AIM OS

**Complete immediately after session**

### SOAP Note Format:

**S - Subjective:**
- Patient's report of symptoms
- Pain level (0-10)
- Activities affected

**O - Objective:**
- Assessment findings
- Treatment provided
- Exercises prescribed

**A - Assessment:**
- Clinical impression
- Progress evaluation

**P - Plan:**
- Next session goals
- Home exercise program
- Expected timeline

**Use AI documentation assistance if available**

---

## SOP-CL-005: PATIENT DISCHARGE PROTOCOL

**When patient recovers:**

1. **Final assessment**
   - Confirm full recovery
   - Test functional movements

2. **Prevention advice**
   - Injury prevention strategies
   - Warning signs to watch

3. **Maintenance exercises**
   - Ongoing strength program
   - Mobility routine

4. **Encourage review**
   > "We'd love your feedback on Google to help others find us"

5. **Encourage referrals**
   > "If you know anyone dealing with injury, we'd love to help them too"

# SECTION 3: GYM INTEGRATION SOP

Because the clinic operates inside Evolve Strength South Commons, collaboration with trainers is critical.

---

## SOP-GI-001: TRAINER REFERRAL WORKFLOW

### When Trainer Notices Injury:

1. **Trainer refers client**
   - Using referral QR code
   - Digital referral form

2. **Front desk intake**
   - Record referral source
   - Note trainer name
   - Thank trainer later

3. **Physiotherapist consultation**
   - Evaluate injury
   - Provide treatment plan

4. **Trainer feedback**
   - Send trainer summary:
     - Injury diagnosis
     - Restrictions
     - Recommended modifications
     - Clearance for training

**This strengthens collaboration**

---

## SOP-GI-002: TRAINER COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL

### After Treating Trainer's Client:

**Send brief update to trainer:**

> "Hi [Trainer Name],
>
> I saw [Client Name] today for [injury]. Here's what you need to know:
>
> Diagnosis: [brief explanation]
> Restrictions: [specific movements to avoid]
> Modifications: [suggested training adjustments]
> Timeline: [expected recovery]
>
> Let me know if you have questions.
>
> [Your Name], PT"

**This builds strong referral relationships**
